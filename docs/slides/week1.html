...air pressure

At microscopic level air consists of individual, tiny particles.

Each particle of air travels through space with some velocity in a more or less random direction.

--> Increase the flow of particles

As these particles travel, they collide with eacth other...

they also colide with objects in space, like walls...

Air pressure is the number of collisions between air molecules at any given time.

You can think of this as the "thinkness" of the air.

...air pressure waves

Sound is a longitudinal wave, like a spring

You may also hear sound waves called "compression waves". Air is compressed and de-compressed.

These are similar to the initial waves of an earthquake

Digital Sound

If acousting (real) sound is pressure waves in the air, what's in the computer?

zeroes and ones

that's it

Ok, but actually...

Sound pressure waves are converted to electric signals via a microphone

That electrical signal is "sampled" (measured) several thousand times a second

And those measurements are saved to a file, as bytes in a "bytestream"

Those audio files are used for playback or editing

We use DAWs (Digital Audio Workstations) to edit sound
